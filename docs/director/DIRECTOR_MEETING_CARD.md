# Director Meeting Card (15-Minute Teams Demo)

## Objective
Show leadership value in 15 minutes: **visibility, accountability, and action** from one role-based command center.

## 0:00–1:30 | Executive Opening (Read this)
"Thank you for the time today. In this demo, I’ll show how OCSS Command Center gives leadership one place to monitor performance, quality risk, and workload pressure across teams."

"You’ll see three outcomes: (1) real-time KPI visibility, (2) Team Performance Analytics by role-appropriate scope, and (3) one-click QA exports for executive review packets."

"The key update is parity across executive roles—Director, Deputy Director, Department Manager, and Senior Administrative Officer now share aligned KPI and analytics capabilities, with role-based scope controls."

## 1:30–4:30 | KPI & Metrics
**Action:** Open KPI tab in Director view.

**Say:**
- "This is our leadership operating picture: completion rate, timeliness, backlog, and escalation aging."
- "These metrics show where intervention is needed before issues become compliance risk."

**Transition line:**
"Next, I’ll show how we isolate performance quickly by organization level."

## 4:30–7:30 | Team Performance Analytics
**Action:** Move to Team Performance Analytics and switch scope levels (Agency → Department → Unit if available).

**Say:**
- "This view turns high-level metrics into team-level accountability."
- "Progress bars show individual/team completion patterns so supervisors and managers can coach early."
- "Leadership can identify where support is needed without waiting for month-end reporting."

**Transition line:**
"Now I’ll show how this becomes a briefing-ready deliverable in one click."

## 7:30–10:00 | Leadership QA Exports
**Action:** From KPI tab, run leadership export (Excel/Word).

**Say:**
- "Exports are available directly from KPI for executive roles."
- "The packet includes KPI summaries and QA flag rollups—FAIL, WARN, INFO, OK—with top failure reasons."
- "This gives us consistent evidence for leadership reviews and follow-up actions."

**Transition line:**
"Visibility matters only if we can act quickly, so here’s the operational response side."

## 10:00–12:00 | Operational Action
**Action:** Briefly show assignment/reassignment and alert acknowledgment path.

**Say:**
- "When risk appears, supervisors/managers can reassign and clear ownership immediately."
- "This closes the loop between insight and execution."

**Transition line:**
"I’ll close with what this enables at the leadership level."

## 12:00–14:00 | Leadership Value Summary
**Say:**
- "For leadership, this means faster decisions with cleaner evidence."
- "For operations, this means earlier intervention and clearer accountability."
- "For oversight, this means exportable QA/KPI packets with consistent structure."

## 14:00–15:00 | Decision Close (Read this)
"If this aligns with your expectations, I recommend a 30-day pilot with weekly executive packet reviews."

"Pilot success criteria: improved on-time completion, reduced aging escalations, and positive QA trend movement in target teams."

"With your approval, we can begin that cadence next week."

---

## Likely Questions (Fast Answers)
- **How is this different from current reporting?**
  - "Current reporting is fragmented and delayed; this unifies live KPI, team analytics, and QA exports in one workflow."
- **Is access controlled by role?**
  - "Yes, role-based visibility enforces unit/department/agency scope appropriately."
- **How does it reduce risk?**
  - "Escalation aging and QA failure trends surface early, enabling targeted intervention."
- **What’s the first measurable win?**
  - "Faster detection of low-performing segments and quicker reassignment/ownership actions."

## Teams Fallback (If Lag/Issue)
"I’ll switch to the prepared export packet to show the same KPI and QA outputs while the app reloads."

"The core capability remains: one leadership lens, scoped drill-down, and executable follow-through."
